A French court has launched a probe into the former prime minister and two ex cabinet ministers ' handling of the coronavirus crisis , the Paris prosecutor 's office told CNN on Friday . 
A judicial enquiry will examine how former Prime Minister Edouard Philippe , who resigned as part of the widely expected government reshuffle on Friday , and two former health ministers — Agnes Buzyn and her successor Olivier Véran — managed the Covid 19 outbreak in France . 
Buzyn stepped down in February . 
The inquiry will begin at the Court of Justice of the Republic after the Petitions and Complaints committee upheld nine complaints against the ex ministers . 
The committee is part of the CJR and examines the complaints to decide whether to pursue prosecution . 
Complaints were filed by private individuals and doctors , the Paris prosecutor 's office told CNN .
