A man photographed fleeing smoke and debris as the south tower of the World Trade Center crumbled just a block away on September 11 , 2001 , has died from coronavirus , his family said . 
The Palm Beach Post reported that Stephen Cooper , an electrical engineer from New York who lived part time in the Delray Beach , Florida area , died 28 March at Delray Medical Center due to Covid 19 . 
He was 78 . 
The photo , captured by an Associated Press photographer , was published in newspapers and magazines around the world and is featured at the 9/11 Memorial Museum in New York . 
" He did not even know the photograph was taken , " said Janet Rashes , Cooper ’s partner of 33 years . 
" All of a sudden , he ’s looking in Time magazine one day and he sees himself and says , ‘ Oh my God . 
That ’s me . ’ 
He was amazed . 
Could not believe it."The photo shows Cooper , who was 60 at the time , with a manila envelope tucked under his left arm . 
He and several other men were in a desperate sprint as a wall of debris from the collapsing tower loomed behind them .
