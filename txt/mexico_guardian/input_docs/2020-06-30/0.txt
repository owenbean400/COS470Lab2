Three people in a camp for asylum seekers on the Mexico US border have tested positive , marking the first cases in a settlement that advocates have long viewed as vulnerable amid the pandemic . 
Since confirmed cases in Mexico began rising in March , advocates and government officials have worried about the potential for an outbreak in the Matamoros camp , where an estimated 2,000 people live in tents on the banks of the Rio Grande river . 
Michele Heisler , the medical director at US based non profit Physicians for Human Rights , said : For months , public health experts have sounded the alarm that people in these under resourced migrant encampments and shelters ... face disproportionate risks from Covid 19 .
