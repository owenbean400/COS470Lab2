A second round of stimulus payments is on the negotiating table in Washington , but some of the 160 million Americans who got money the first time could be left out . 
Instead , the Trump administration is pushing for a more limited approach . 
That 's more likely to garner Republican support in Congress , where lawmakers are expected to consider another economic spending bill in late July . 
" There 's a lot of discussions going on . 
Probably , we would want to target those folks who lost their jobs and are most in need , " said White House Economic Adviser Larry Kudlow in an interview with Fox Business Tuesday . 
His remarks followed comments from President Donald Trump earlier in the week that suggested Americans would be getting a second round of checks that would be " very generous . "
