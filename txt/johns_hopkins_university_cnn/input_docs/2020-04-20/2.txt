As of midday Sunday , Singapore had reported another 596 cases of coronavirus , according to the country 's Ministry of Health . 
Singapore has now reported at least 6,588 cases , including 11 deaths , according to a count from Johns Hopkins University . 
Who are the new cases ? 
Some 544 of the new cases were detected in migrant workers living in dormitories . 
There were also 30 new cases found in the wider community . 
Where are the people who are infected ? 
According to the ministry , a total of 768 people have fully recovered from the infection and been discharged from hospitals or community isolation facilities . 
Another 2,921 confirmed cases are still in hospital , with most stable or improving . 
According to the ministry , 22 are in critical condition in the intensive care unit . 
Some 2,888 cases that are clinically well but still tested positive for coronavirus have been isolated and cared for at community facilities . 
The background : Less than a month ago , Singapore was being hailed as one of the countries that had got its coronavirus response right . 
Encouragingly for the rest of the world , the city state seemed to have suppressed cases without imposing the restrictive lockdown measures endured by millions elsewhere . 
And then the second wave hit , hard . 
So what went wrong ? 
The answer appears to lie in overlooked clusters of cases among migrant workers living in cramped dormitories and an underestimation of the speed at which those infections could spread through a city where lockdown measures had not been put in place .
