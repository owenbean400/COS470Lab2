As Madrid ’s spring evenings warm into summer nights , cinemagoers are parking up to watch Grease . 
In Munich , they are taking al fresco seats to follow the adventures of a communist kangaroo with a penchant for boozy chocolates , and in Prague they are witnessing a croaky vigilante work out some profound childhood traumas . 
As Europe begins to stir from its Covid 19 lockdown , people bloated by two month boxset binges have a new way to feed their entertainment needs as they emerge , blinking , into the daylight . 
Or , rather , the twilight . 
Openair cinemas in Spain , Germany , the Czech Republic and Greece have reopened in recent days , albeit at reduced capacity and with the novel strictures of physical distancing . 
Our reporters in the three countries have investigated Europe ’s new entertainment boom .
