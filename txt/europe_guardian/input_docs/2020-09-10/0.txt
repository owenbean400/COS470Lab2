Greek authorities on the island of Lesbos are racing to find refugees who had tested positive for Covid 19 before a series of devastating fires forced thousands to flee Europe ’s biggest migrant camp , Helena Smith reports . 
Health officials have rushed 19,000 test kits to the north eastern Aegean island amid fears of a surge in coronavirus cases . 
" It ’s a very dangerous , very explosive situation , " Efstratios Tzimis , Mytilene ’s deputy mayor , told the Guardian . 
" And it ’s making all of us crazed . 
On the one hand there ’s coronavirus , on the other thousands of desperate , hungry people . " 
Only eight of the more than three dozen refugees diagnosed with the virus have been found . 
Authorities say they have been quarantined in a special area with close family members .
