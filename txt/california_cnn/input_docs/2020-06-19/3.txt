Californians who do not wear face masks in public settings can be fined after Gov . 
Gavin Newsom issued a statewide order Thursday requiring residents wear the coverings when outside of home . 
Newsom ’s office today told CNN that the mask order is enforceable if necessary , as a misdemeanor carrying a potential financial penalty and other penalties . 
State agencies like the Division of Occupational Safety and Health could also take action over violations of the face mask order . 
The governor ’s order requires face coverings in " high risk " settings , including inside any indoor public space , taking public transportation , or seeking medical care .
