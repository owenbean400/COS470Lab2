As many as 50,000 Americans may need their government ’s help in getting home , the US State Department says ; a huge increase on its previous estimate of only about 13,500 . 
Ian Brownlee , the head of the department ’s repatriation task force , said it had brought back more than 9,000 people from 28 countries , while another 9,000 are set to return on 66 flights over the next nine days . 
But the scale of the problem is straining resources . 
In as much as this is a truly unprecedented event , the State Department ’s capacity to do this is being strained . 
We ’re talking to the Department of Defense as to whether they can potentially help us out in lining up aircraft .
