There are 1.2 million people across New York who have filed unemployment claims over the past five weeks , Melissa De Rosa , secretary to Gov . 
Andrew Cuomo , told reporters Thursday afternoon . 
Of those claims , 400,000 were filed this past week . 
To give a sense of comparison , " during the entirety of the 2008 crash , New York state lost 300,000 jobs , " De Rosa said . 
The Department of Labor has completed 925,000 claims and 275,000 others are outstanding , according to De Rosa . 
Some of the 275,000 claims are from last week and some of those claims date back to the middle of March , she said .
